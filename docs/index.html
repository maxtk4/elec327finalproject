ELEC 327 Payload

A Robotic payload designed for the SAE Aero Design Advanced 2025 Competition

Enabling a VTOL aircraft to deliver and capture a payload simply by landing and taking off

Magpie

This is Magpie, the aircraft designed to function with this payload. It first takes off carrying a payload attached to the velcro plate below it. When Magpie approaches the designated landing zone where it is attempting to deliver the payload, It lands vertically. The payload senses Magpie has landed, and retracts its velcro top, separating it from the bottom of Magpie's attachment plate. Once the aircraft has flown away, the payload once agai extends this plate, such that another aircraft can pick up the payload by landing on top of the velcro.

Payload Design

The payload is designed around an MSPM0G3507 microcontroller, connected to Neopixel LEDs, a linear servo, and two limit switches. The microcontroller and surrounding circuitry are powered via two buck converters outputing 3.3V for the microcontroller and 5V for the servo and Neopixels. The buck regulators are powered with 7.4 Volts nominal from a 2 cell lithium polymer battery. The schematic and layout are in the github repository for the project, however we were unable to get the MCU to flash due to the passive components being wired incorrectly.

Hitec Linear Servo

The payload uses this Hitec servo to extend and retract the payload. It communicates through varying length PWM commands, which are configured by setting the CC_01 register, which changes the duty cycle of the PWM output from the PWM register.
